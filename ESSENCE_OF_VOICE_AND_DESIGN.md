# The Essence of Ciechanowski — Voice & Design

What Bartosz Ciechanowski (ciechanow.ski) actually does, reverse-engineered from the full
corpus: all 22 posts downloaded and read (~155,000 words, 908 interactive figures).
Study texts: `research/ciechanowski/`. Four full close-reading reports:
`research/reports/`. Claims below are measured or quoted, not remembered.

**The one-line thesis** (from the evolution study): his 2019 transformation was not a
style change but *an audience change executed with total discipline* — he kept every
2014 instinct (puns, failure-first, beauty-talk, foreshadowing, "Final Words") and
swapped exactly two things: **the reader** (Apple developer → anyone) and **the
evidence** (his console output → your hands on a slider).

---

## 1. The rhythm (measured)

- **One figure per ~142 words.** Median gap: **2 paragraphs** (~100 words); 1–3
  paragraphs covers 80% of gaps; figures adjacent without prose only 3% of the time.
- **Sentences average ~22 words** (p90 = 34) — long, even, comma-chained legato. No
  punchy fragments, no one-sentence drama paragraphs. The signature cadence is **long
  build → short verdict**: "One could be tempted to use an arbitrary shape for the
  individual weighting functions… The results are completely janky."
- **Paragraphs ~50 words** — two to three sentences, one move each.
- Posts: 3,000–16,500 words, 21–120 figures, 6–16 sections. Length grew monotonically
  (gears 2020: 30 figures → moon 2024: 120) but words-per-figure stays in a tight band
  (84–179). The masterpieces are *more* of the same rhythm, never a different rhythm.
- **Controls: ~0.75–1.0 per figure, and one knob per figure is the law** — 78% of
  figures have ≤1 control; ~97% of all controls are sliders; the single most common
  slider is **time-speed** (slow motion is his default epistemic instrument). Knob
  budget escalates with reader competence: finales may earn 3–4 sliders, flagged with
  a wink ("Behold the sliders galore!").
- Transitions are liturgical: *"in the demonstration below"* ×262, *"Let's"* ×369,
  *"Notice"* ×313. The sameness is deliberate — figures carry the novelty.
- **Zero rhetorical questions** (0 question marks in 36k words of the mechanism posts).
  Wonder is declarativized: "It may be a little puzzling why…"
- **Exclamation marks: ~2 per 36,000 words**, spent only on counterintuitive truth
  ("this is exactly how a bicycle turns!").
- Section titles: plain nouns (*Power. Gears. Air. Pressure.*) with occasional smuggled
  puns ("Seeing the Matrix" → "The Matrix Seeing" for matrix inversion).

## 2. Voice

**The three-persona pronoun system** — the deepest voice rule:
- **"We" builds** the physics ("we'll assemble the keyless works") — reader and author
  as one expedition. ~110–212 uses per post.
- **"You" experiments** — always permissive, never imperative: "you can drag," "you can
  witness," never "click the slider."
- **"I" rigs the stage** — first person appears almost exclusively to confess a
  visualization intervention: "I adjusted the lengths of the arrows to prevent them from
  visually overlapping"; "I temporarily removed the screw to make things easier to see";
  "to spare myself a 3D modeling exercise." **Trust is built by confessing the lie in
  every visualization.**

The rest of the fingerprint, quote-backed:

- **Jargon protocol: phenomenon first, name second, always.** "…is known as / so-called
  / called" (42+ uses): "What we're witnessing here is known as a stall." The term is
  the *last* thing you learn about a concept. Etymology arrives as a reward ("in Latin
  *tangere* means 'to touch'"). Plus the up-front amnesty: "you shouldn't feel pressured
  to remember them – the names and parts will be color-coded for easy reference."
- **Emotion lives in discourse markers, not adjectives.** "Unfortunately," introduces
  the failure of the naive design (16×/post); "Thankfully," introduces the engineered
  rescue; "However" is the plot-twist operator (141×). These are his plot devices.
- **Parts are animate; the reader never is.** Springs "want to expand," forces are
  "eager," the escape wheel "escapes from that jail," "the air somehow senses the
  presence of the body." But reader competence is axiomatic — he never says "you might
  be confused."
- **Discovery is credited to the reader**: "You may have noticed a subtle dance
  between…" — he writes as if watching your hands, and retroactively attributes his own
  observations to you.
- **Pre-empting misperception is a signature move**: "Don't be misled by the frozen
  arrows, the wind is actually blowing there"; "This is not a mistake."
- **"May seem X, but Y"** (14×): pre-empts both intimidation and triviality — "It may
  seem that we only made things worse, but in fact we simplified things greatly."
- **One load-bearing metaphor per section**, introduced, used hard, and audited: the
  balance is "the beating heart of a watch"; still air is "a swarm of bees" — followed
  by "An imperfect, but convenient analogy…" Analogies never leave the physical world;
  they are always *simpler mechanical systems*, usually given their own figure (tennis
  balls for pressure, a marble on a landscape for ∇p, a bar on ropes for tidal torque).
- **Household anchors, one per concept**: hand out the car window, spoon in honey,
  uncooked spaghetti, an aluminum can, a credit card dragged to scale on screen, "the
  next time you watch a movie pay close attention to the shape of out-of-focus
  highlights."
- **Awe-numbers are dessert, never vegetables** — deployed only after intuition exists:
  an 80 nm cube holding twelve thousand particles; 1030 mph average particle speed; ten
  billion collisions per second; "31,508,400 wave cycles per bit." Numbers are staged
  as drama ("Sixty four. Times. Faster." — a habit visible since 2014).
- **Deadpan humor 2–3× per post, at his own expense, never the reader's**: "I would
  advise against relying on them when building an airworthy airplane"; "world's most
  primitive drawing application"; "crude oil, or even orange juice."
- **Transitions are camera moves**: "Let's zoom away from the world of microscopic
  particles"; "let's raise above the ground"; "Let's leave the world of particles behind
  for the last time."
- **Hedges are calibrated, not defensive** — "more or less," "roughly," "at least in
  principle" attach only to genuinely approximate claims; hard claims get none ("Those
  assertions are not true" — flatly debunking the GPS-relativity myth).
- **Present tense, always.** The machine operates *now*; past tense is reserved for
  history sentences.
- **What he never does**: numbered figure references; captions; rhetorical questions;
  "obviously/clearly/trivially"; pop culture in body prose (puns hide in section titles
  only); inline citations (sources quarantined to Further Reading); motivational filler;
  apologies for difficulty; reader blame; an equation before its interactive; an
  unresolved plant.

## 3. Design grammar

- **Colored vocabulary is the load-bearing system.** Prose words are printed in the
  color of the thing they name — `{color_red|barrel}`, `{lift|lift}`, `{aoa|angle of
  attack}` — 51 → 605 spans per post as the system matured (2021→2024), up to 47
  distinct color classes in one post. The word *is* the pointer; there is no "see
  figure 3." It extends beyond nouns to **actions** ({color_pull|pull the crown}),
  **operators** ({alpha_operator|source-over}), and **variables in equations** — the
  unknowns of GPS's equation system are typeset in the colors of their on-canvas
  counterparts. The reader's eye does the type-checking.
- **Figures have no captions. None.** Zero `figcaption` elements in 22 posts. The
  sentence above sets up; the sentence below reads out. Figures are chrome-free.
- **Ring structure.** Figure 1 is the *finished artifact* plus an explicit IOU ("This
  may all feel quite overwhelming at the moment, but we'll eventually see how all these
  pieces fit together"); the final figure is the hero again, now understood — curves'
  Fig 90 *is* Fig 1's mask; airfoil's camber slider "finally recreated the asymmetric
  shape we first saw." **The article is a proof that terminates at its own epigraph.**
- **A persistent protagonist with stable color identity.** One artifact survives the
  whole post (the movement, the airfoil "the protagonist of this article," one
  red-sphere/green-sphere scene photographed for 56 figures). Barrel = red from fig 6
  to fig 93. Once a visual noun is taught, it is never redesigned. New concept ≠ new
  object; new concept = **new overlay on the same object** — the same scene recurs
  gaining arrows → markers → colors → force decompositions. Representation change is
  isolated as the only variable.
- **One-delta figures**: consecutive demos differ by exactly one element (one more
  sphere, one more bounce, one more dimension). The diff *is* the concept.
- **Dual-pane simultaneity**: world+map, scene+photograph, 3D object+projection,
  curve+weight-plot, ground-camera+car-camera. One canvas shows reality, the twin shows
  the representation, one interaction drives both — *the mapping between them is the
  lesson*.
- **2D for measurement, 3D for phenomena.** He drops a dimension whenever a value must
  be *read*; 3D appears only when the phenomenon is irreducibly spatial, always with
  drag-to-orbit.
- **Failure states are playable.** Sliders deliberately extend into wrong regimes
  (pressure too high → flow reverses; cargo too high → capsize; AoA too high → stall).
  The reader finds the boundary by crossing it.
- **Control lifecycle**: introduce manual control → let the reader struggle ("After
  some trial and error you may get pretty close") → declare the toil unscalable ("a
  daunting endeavor") → derive the formalism *as relief* → automate or remove the
  control. (Visible as early as 2014: "We're going to get rid of the slider.")
- **Reader-respect furniture**: global animation pause ("if you want to save power"),
  metric/imperial word toggles inline in prose, click/tap adaptive text, restart
  buttons, minimaps when a demo is "destructive," volume warnings before audio.
- **The page itself as specimen** when possible: "Almost everything you see on this
  website was blended using this mode"; the site's white background doubles as "a
  bright overcast sky" in the camera post.

## 4. The pedagogy engine

The deep structure of every post is **a chain of failures repaired**:

1. **Broken version first — the failure is simulated, never described.** Marker phrase:
   "One could (naively) assume/think…" Hand glued to the mainspring barrel spins wildly;
   made-up pressure fields let air flow *through* the airfoil ("This is clearly
   wrong!"); steer-left-to-go-left tips the bicycle over. The failure specifies the
   requirements for the next invention.
2. **Section-final savior sentence.** Nearly every section ends by naming the next
   component as the answer to a freshly created problem: "This is where gears come in."
   / "we'll do this with the escapement." The article is a chain of engineered
   cliff-resolutions; sections end with forward hooks, not summaries.
3. **Arithmetic creates the problem the next mechanism solves**: 40 hours → 2400
   rotations → 343:1 → "these proportions are ridiculous" (shown with a zoom slider) →
   therefore a gear *train*. The reader feels the constraint before receiving the
   solution.
4. **Representation before phenomenon.** Airfoil teaches how to *see* flow (arrows,
   drifting markers, color maps) for a whole section before any physics; Moon has an
   entire section on how to observe the sky. Nothing moves until the reader can read
   the display.
5. **Micro → macro, then formal abandonment.** Velocity, pressure, viscosity, no-slip
   are each grounded in particle statistics — then he explicitly cashes out: "This fact
   lets us abandon the molecules and their collisions yet again." Continuum concepts
   are never axioms.
6. **The reader plays inverse-solver before the equation is named.** Hand-tune the
   pressure field region by region against two stated constraints; only after you've
   *been* the solver does he say: "The quite informal description of these balances…
   can be formalized mathematically using the Navier–Stokes equations." The equation
   arrives as *the formalization of something you already did*.
7. **Verbal feedback loops as ODE substitutes**: "If the pressure is too low… If it is
   too high… The system balances itself yet again" — the same syntactic template
   repeated three times (nose/top/tail), and again for tidal bulges in Moon. The
   repetition is rhetorical scaffolding.
8. **Just-in-time concepts.** Viscosity — which every textbook front-loads — arrives at
   66% of Airfoil, only after stall creates a *need* for it. Lift is derived first from
   an effectively inviscid pressure argument; viscosity exists to explain lift's
   *failure*.
9. **Misconception-proofing by omission.** Verified by search: **"Bernoulli,"
   "streamline," "circulation," and "equal transit time" appear zero times** in 14,500
   words about lift. He'd rather not name a concept than let a wrong version of it into
   the reader's head. (Further Reading confirms it's deliberate — he praises McLean for
   debunking exactly those explanations.) Negative results he can't simulate get flat
   declarative debunks.
10. **Chekhov's lever / planted payoffs.** Parts and demos are planted with an IOU and
    redeemed sections later ("we're also going to attach a little lever that we will
    get back to at a later point"); "Recall that…" is the standard redemption operator.
    Airfoil's fig 2 (viscosity cube) resolves at fig 63, four thousand words later. He
    keeps visible books on what's owed, and every debt is paid.
11. **Scare-then-decompose.** Show the full complexity once, early, to justify the slow
    path ("it's easy to get overwhelmed with all the arrows… We'll eventually
    understand all of these forces").
12. **Simplify with a contract, then un-simplify.** "In our toy engine the valves
    magically opened and closed on their own, so let's see how it can actually be done
    in practice." The symmetric airfoil is borrowed with a stated reason and the
    asymmetric one returned at the end. Every simplification is disclosed at the moment
    it's made, and every loop is closed.
13. **Boundary-value sanity checks after every formalism**: "plugging progress t equal
    to 1 simply returns the point B"; "When eccentricity is 0, an ellipse becomes a
    perfect circle."
14. **Enumerated harvest after rich demos**: "There are many interesting observations
    to be done here:" + 3–4 bullets — he decompresses his own dense figures.
15. **Cross-post curriculum reuse by re-teaching, not linking.** Sound (2022) built the
    particle/pressure kit that Airfoil (2024) redeploys demo-for-demo. Each post stays
    self-contained.

## 5. Math handling — the protocol

His relationship with equations is *budgeted*, and it collapsed over time by choice:
gears (2020) has ~20 display equations; mechanical watch (2022) has **zero**; airfoil
(2024) has exactly one (Reynolds). F = ma is "granted a ceremony" in Bicycle as a
stated exception. Fluids get the *least* math of all — he refuses to fake tractability.

But when he *does* do math (curves-and-surfaces is the model), the protocol is exact:

1. **Interactive first** — the property is observed in a figure.
2. **Plain words** state the relationship.
3. **Symbols introduced as a compression, with apology**: "A word 'progress' would make
   the formulas very long so I'll replace it with the traditionally used letter t."
4. **Operational semantics before algebra**: "It may not be immediately clear what it
   means to multiply a point by a number so let's explore that a little" → figure.
5. **The formula**, with every symbol already seen on canvas, color-bound to its pixels.
6. **Boundary-value sanity check** immediately after.
7. **Every coefficient eventually gets its own interactive plot** (the draggable
   "weight hat" surfaces; little triangle glyphs on (1−t) and t matching their plots).
8. **Notational refusal at scale**: symbols appear only where they'll be *reused*;
   "the arithmetical procedures aren't particularly important here" otherwise.

Substitutes for equations elsewhere: interactive equilibria, the feedback-loop prose
template, empirical plots with sliders (lift-vs-AoA), physical constructions (the
involute drawn by unwinding a real string), and outsourcing rigor to Further Reading
(Navier–Stokes → Lorena Barba's *12 Steps*; the Further Reading list is, in the words
of one of our reports, "the silent apology").

## 6. Structure: openings, arcs, endings

**Openings** are cold, atmospheric, two sentences, then an immediately playable hero.
Hook taxonomies observed: personal fascination ("I've always been fascinated by
mechanical gears"), anachronism-wonder (watch), ubiquity+mystery ("omnipresent yet
cryptic" — engine, GPS), embodied delight (bicycle), anti-hype concession
("Transparency may not seem particularly exciting"), deliberate incomprehensibility
(tesseract's unexplainable hero), the-familiar-is-secretly-complex (earth-and-sun).
Common denominator: **a wonder gap between daily familiarity and hidden mechanism**,
plus an explicit IOU on the hero figure.

**Arcs** are either a *failure chain* (mechanisms), a *constraint-relaxation ladder*
(GPS removes one impracticality per section), or *push-each-representation-until-it-
breaks* (curves). All three are the same move at different grains: **the current
system's visible inadequacy names the next section.**

**Endings are invariant**: **Further Reading** (each source praised in 2–3 specific
sentences — "The site truly is one of the internet's gems") → **Final Words** (a title
he has used since 2014) → an "I find it fascinating/captivating/inspiring…" sentence →
a **re-enchantment benediction** returning the object to daily life: "Perhaps the next
time you're on a bicycle, you'll be able to conceptualize some of those invisible
forces that make the ride so enjoyable." The closing thesis of the whole corpus:
**understanding increases wonder**.

## 7. What the evolution teaches

The 2014 era (Obj-C internals, 50 code blocks per post, "Every self-respecting
programmer knows…") already contained the puns, the failure-first pedagogy, the beauty
vocabulary, the foreshadowing discipline, even interactive demos (bezier, Feb 2014).
What changed on Jan 1, 2019 (*Exposing Floating Point*, the hinge document, half old /
half new): the customer became the general reader, and the evidence moved from **his
console to your hands**. Code blocks went 50 → 11 → 0 and never returned; question
marks went 15 → 0; "I" the investigator became "we" the expedition; subjects switched
from things that rot with SDK releases to things that cannot expire (CIE 1931, IEEE
754, gears, the Moon).

Lesson for us: the style is portable to any audience *decision*. Our decision differs
from his (see METHODOLOGY.md §0): our reader wants to *earn the equations*, so our
evidence is the reader's hands **and** the derivation.

## 8. Critique — what the style costs (and where we diverge)

1. **No quantitative exit.** After 14.5k words on airfoils the reader can compute
   nothing — no lift coefficient, unlabeled plot axes. "Superb intuition, zero
   engineering traction." *Our divergence #1: we cash out into the actual equations,
   using his own curves-and-surfaces protocol (§5) to do it.*
2. **Interaction is mostly confirmation, not experiment.** Nothing the reader can get
   *wrong*; the "one could assume…" traps are sprung by the author; no prediction is
   ever solicited before a reveal. *Divergence #2: ask for a committed prediction
   before marquee reveals.*
3. **No skim path, no waypoints, no retrieval structure.** Zero recaps, no TOC, no
   consolidating tables; a reader who bails mid-post leaves with nothing collected.
   Bail points are measurable: they're the **figure-density droughts** (GPS's
   Navigation Message: ~600 words, zero figures). *Divergence #3: short "what you now
   hold" waypoints at act boundaries — kept un-preachy.*
4. **Simulation epistemics are opaque.** "The computer models used here are quite
   simplified" is honest but unfalsifiable — he never names the solver, so the reader
   can't tell physics from numerical artifact. *Divergence #4: we name our schemes and
   their stability conditions in-post; the solver is part of our subject matter.*
5. **Color is a single point of failure** — the load-bearing prose↔canvas binding
   vanishes for colorblind readers, screen readers, and print. There is no static
   fallback path. *We adopt the device and owe it a fallback story.*
6. **Metronomic pacing flattens emphasis**; profound moments arrive at the same tempo
   as housekeeping. Mid-post "inventory" stretches (watch's Date/Keyless sections) and
   prerequisite detours (camera's Waves section) are where momentum dies.
7. **Endings are a stamped template** — read in sequence, the benedictions become
   interchangeable. The one place a synthesis diagram would land, he goes lyrical.
8. **The cost is the moat**: 80–120 bespoke figures per post ≈ one post per year. Any
   imitation must budget figures deliberately (reuse-with-overlay amortizes; our
   `<Sim>` framework is the scaling bet) or die of scope.
